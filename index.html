What is Disney World?

Walt Disney World Resort, located in Orlando, Florida, is the most visited vacation resort in the world. Spanning over 25,000 acres, it's home to four incredible theme parks, two water parks, numerous hotels, shopping, dining, and entertainment venues.

Since opening in 1971, Disney World has been creating magical experiences for millions of guests from around the world. Each park offers unique attractions, shows, and experiences that bring Disney stories to life.

Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned Disney fan, there's always something new to discover and experience at the most magical place on earth.

Plan Your Trip

Planning Your Visit

The best times to visit Disney World are typically:

  • January and February (after New Year's, before spring break)
  • Late April through early May
  • September through early November (avoiding holidays)

Avoid peak times like summer, holidays, and spring break for shorter wait times and better weather.

Single Park Tickets: Access to one park per day. Best for first-time visitors or longer stays.

Park Hopper: Visit multiple parks in one day. Great for returning visitors who want flexibility.

Park Hopper Plus: Includes water parks and other experiences. Perfect for extended stays.

Multi-day tickets offer better value per day. Consider your travel style when choosing.

Genie+: A paid service that allows you to skip the regular standby line at select attractions using Lightning Lane entrances.

Individual Lightning Lane: Separate purchase for the most popular attractions like Star Wars: Rise of the Resistance.

Genie+ pricing varies by date. Purchase through the My Disney Experience app on the day of your visit. Availability is limited.

Minimum: 4-5 days to experience all four theme parks at a comfortable pace.

Recommended: 6-7 days allows time to revisit favorites, explore resorts, and enjoy dining experiences.

Extended Stay: 8+ days for a relaxed vacation with time for water parks, Disney Springs, and resort activities.

Consider adding rest days, especially with young children, to avoid burnout.

On-Site Benefits: Early park entry, free transportation, package delivery, and immersive theming.

Off-Site Options: Often more affordable, more space, and flexibility. You'll need a car or rideshare.

On-site resorts range from value to deluxe. Off-site hotels offer great value, especially for larger families. Consider your budget and priorities.

Getting Around

Transportation

Getting to Disney World

By Air: Orlando International Airport (MCO) is 20 miles away. Disney's Magical Express service is available for on-site guests.

By Car: Located off I-4, easily accessible from major highways. Parking available at all theme parks ($25/day).

Transportation Within Property

  • Monorail: Connects Magic Kingdom, EPCOT, and select resorts
  • Buses: Free transportation to all parks and resorts
  • Boats: Scenic water transportation to select parks and resorts
  • Disney Skyliner: Aerial gondola system connecting EPCOT, Hollywood Studios, and resorts

Parking Information

Standard parking is $25 per day and valid at all four theme parks. Preferred parking ($45-50) is available closer to park entrances. On-site resort guests receive complimentary parking.
